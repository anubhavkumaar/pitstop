# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

GTA V roleplay players on the San Andreas (FiveM) server, browsing on a real phone or desktop browser outside the game. They come here to find Pit Stop / Benny's: see what services exist, what they cost, who's on the crew, whether the work is legit (work log, reviews, gallery), and how to actually book something in-game (the Services app flow). Around the July 1st launch specifically, they're also here to learn about Benny's opening and the car giveaway. There's a secondary audience too: the crew themselves, signing in to `/staff`, `/admin`, `/bennys`, `/soochi` to manage bookings, log jobs, and run the giveaway draw. For both audiences, this site doubles as the business "flexing" inside the RP community, not just a utility.

## Product Purpose

Pit Stop was a freelance civilian crew doing mobile car repair, refuel, and detailing in Los Santos. They've now opened a real, physical shop: **Benny's Original Motor Works**. The site exists to announce that shift, sell the services (still freelance-flavored: pickup/drop-off, flat fees, fast turnaround), prove the crew is legit and active (work log, reviews, gallery), and drive bookings plus the launch-day giveaway traffic. Success looks like: a visitor immediately reads this as one real garage (not two brands stapled together), understands what Benny's is and that it's opening July 1st, and knows how to book or show up.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **blue-collar, theatrical, handbuilt.**

This is a working garage that also knows how to put on a show, the way a roleplay server's "real businesses" lean into a bit of performance and spectacle on top of being genuinely useful. Benny's Original Motor Works leads: vintage Americana hot-rod shop energy, red/white outline-lettered signage, script accents, chrome and pinstripe details, warm workshop-light contrast against the dark. Pit Stop becomes the secondary mark, the origin story and legacy name (the freelance crew that earned the right to open a real shop), not erased but no longer the dominant visual voice.

Voice: specific and workshop-direct, not corporate-franchise. Like one named shop with a personality, not an AutoZone template.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS/AI-template landing page tells: gradient text, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section, numbered `01/02/03` section scaffolding, identical icon+heading+text card grids, the hero-metric template, glassmorphism used decoratively, side-stripe accent borders on cards.
- Soulless franchise-chain auto shop feel (Jiffy Lube / AutoZone genericness). This should read as one specific, opinionated garage.
- Anything that reads as "AI made this." Explicit user priority: it should look cool, different, and like deliberate human craft, not a template.
- Don't let Benny's swallow Pit Stop's history entirely. The narrative is continuity ("the crew you used to call now has a shop"), not a rebrand that pretends Pit Stop never existed.

## Design Principles

1. **The signage is the brand mark, not a logo bolted onto someone else's layout.** Benny's actual lettering, its outline-font construction, its red/white/navy palette, and its arched-banner shape should drive real layout and typographic decisions, not just sit in a nav corner.
2. **Pit Stop is the origin story, Benny's is the present.** Keep the freelance-crew legacy visible (founding story, the "we come to you" roots) as part of the lore, folded under the new shop rather than deleted.
3. **Earn trust as a real, specific business.** Copy, pricing, crew bios, and work-log evidence should read like one actual garage's site, not generic template marketing copy.
4. **Function-first where the job is operational, expressive where the brand is on show.** The redesign covers staff/admin/giveaway tools too, but those screens should inherit the new palette and type system while keeping speed and clarity as the priority; the theatrical, bolder moves belong on the public-facing pages.
5. **No AI-template tells.** Actively avoid the category-reflex moves (gradient text, eyebrow kickers, numbered scaffolding, identical card grids, hero-metric template, decorative glassmorphism) — this was named explicitly as a priority.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1, visible keyboard focus states. No additional accommodations requested, but worth flagging proactively: red becomes a primary brand/action color now, used alongside the existing yellow/red status pills in the staff tools, so status meaning must keep using text/icon labels alongside color, never color alone.
